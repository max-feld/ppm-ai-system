# PLG Growth Pulse

A weekly PLG health check that benchmarks performance against industry data and distinguishes signal from noise.

## The problem it solves

PLG motions generate a lot of data and very little insight. Daily signup numbers swing 30-40% on a small base and teams either panic at dips or celebrate noise. Activation funnels get reported without context for whether the numbers are good or bad. Retention curves look alarming to stakeholders who've never seen a PLG retention curve before. And nobody is connecting the dots: signups up but activation down is a lead quality problem, not a growth win.

This skill runs a structured weekly analysis that benchmarks every metric against Poyar's industry data for the relevant ACV band, applies Sharp's light-buyer principle so the team doesn't overreact to normal retention patterns, and delivers specific recommendations instead of directional platitudes.

## The analysis framework

Every weekly check follows four sections:

**1. Health Signal (Red / Yellow / Green).** Overall rating with a one-line justification. Green means the motion is working and improving. Yellow means stable with known issues. Red means something is broken or deteriorating. Determined by: is the activation funnel improving week over week, are key metrics inside Poyar benchmarks, is the retention curve showing signs of flattening for the intended heavy-user cohort, is lead quality holding.

**2. What's Working.** Genuine bright spots with specificity and business impact. Not "signups are up" but "component editing rate improved from 39% to 44%, which means 40+ more users per month reaching the depth where conversion probability rises, and at current pilot conversion rates that's potentially $X more pipeline." Even in tough weeks, finding the signal keeps the growth team moving.

**3. Where to Dig In.** The 2-3 most important things to focus on, ranked by expected impact. For each: what the data says (the specific metric), why it matters (connection to revenue or strategic position), how it compares to benchmarks (Poyar comparison), what to do about it (specific, actionable), and how to measure success (what "better" looks like in 1-2 weeks).

**4. Next Week's Watch List.** 2-3 specific things to track in next week's data. Creates continuity between check-ins and gives the team a focused lens.

## The Poyar benchmark layer

Every metric gets compared against Poyar's benchmark ranges for the relevant ACV band. This is critical because most PLG benchmark data floating around comes from $0-99/month self-serve tools. A $20K+ ACV product running a hybrid sales-assisted PLG motion has fundamentally different conversion economics:

- Signup → activation: 30-50% (below 20% is an onboarding problem)
- Day 7 retention: 10-20% (this looks low to stakeholders used to SaaS NRR curves, but it's normal for PLG)
- PQL → paid conversion (sales-assisted): 15-25% (below 10% is a lead quality or sales follow-up issue)
- Free trial → paid (unassisted): 2-5% (pure self-serve is rare at enterprise ACV, don't optimize against this)

When a metric over- or under-performs a benchmark, the skill surfaces it explicitly: "Activation rate is 42%, in the middle of Poyar's 30-50% benchmark for $20K+ ACV B2B PLG. Healthy." That framing helps the team know whether to celebrate, ignore, or worry.

## The Sharp guardrail

Byron Sharp's research is consistent: in any market, most buyers are light buyers. The same applies to B2B PLG. A retention curve that drops to 12% by Day 7 is not a failed product. It's a normal PLG curve where most users explored, learned, and didn't come back this month. They may return next quarter when a project triggers it.

Before reading low retention as a crisis, the skill asks: Are the right cohorts retaining? (A 12% Day 7 with ICP-fit accounts retaining at 35% is a winning motion.) Is the heavy-user cohort growing? Is the curve flattening or still falling? What's the absolute number of retained users in the high-intent cohort?

This guardrail prevents the team from over-reacting to retention patterns that are within the normal range of how PLG funnels work at enterprise ACV.

## The ICP quality lens

More signups isn't necessarily growth. The skill frames every metric through: "Are we getting more of the right people to the right depth?" A high percentage of personal email signups, for example, isn't a volume win. It's a lead quality signal that needs investigation. At $20K+ ACV, each converted PLG user is worth a lot, so even small activation improvements on the qualified segment move the needle more than doubling unqualified signups.

## What makes this skill compound

Each week builds on the last. The skill references previous weeks' data, tracks whether prior recommendations were acted on and what happened, and calls out inflection points and trend breaks. Pattern recognition across weeks is where the real insights live, not any single snapshot.
